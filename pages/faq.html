Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before booking your first session with Eliza.

Getting started
The first session is a consultation and assessment. Eliza will discuss your goals, fitness history, any injuries or health conditions, and what you want to achieve. You'll do a basic movement assessment so she can understand your starting point and design a programme specifically for you. There's no pressure — it's a relaxed conversation first.
Absolutely not. Eliza works with complete beginners through to competitive athletes. Every programme is built around your current level. Starting from zero is completely normal and nothing to be nervous about.
Eliza is fluent in English, French and Polish, and also speaks some Russian. Sessions can be conducted in whichever language you're most comfortable with — language is never a barrier.
Sessions take place at the gym on Rue du Grand-Pré 58, 1202 Genève. Outdoor sessions around Lac Léman and Geneva's parks can also be arranged depending on the programme and season.
Training & programmes

Nutrition coaching

Pre & post natal fitness
Yes — with the right guidance. Eliza is certified in pre and post natal fitness and designs programmes that are safe and effective for every trimester. Exercise during pregnancy, when properly adapted, has proven benefits for both mother and baby including reduced back pain, better sleep, and faster postnatal recovery.
This depends on the type of birth and individual recovery. Generally, gentle movement can begin within days, but structured exercise is typically introduced from 6 weeks post vaginal birth or 8–12 weeks post C-section, with medical clearance. Eliza will always work within your doctor's guidelines.
Yes. Eliza's post natal programme specifically addresses core rehabilitation, including diastasis recti recovery. This requires a careful, progressive approach — certain exercises need to be avoided initially, and Eliza knows exactly how to safely rebuild core strength after birth.
